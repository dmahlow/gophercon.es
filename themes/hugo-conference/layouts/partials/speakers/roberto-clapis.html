Description

A deep dive on contention: what is it, when is it expensive, and how to tackle it. Some light will be shed on ways to measure the computational power wasted waiting. The trace/block and pprof tools and libraries will be explained with actionable examples. After showing how to find contention issues, some handy ways to address them will be explained: Splitting work: different ways to change common algorithms to not have a centralized state. From channels to mutexes: where it makes sense to make the switch, and why. From mutexes to atomics: the real price of making this step, and how to make an informed choice about going lock-free.
